Overview

We developed a VR game for Google Cardboard to train users on how to prepare for typhoon. I took a role as a team leader and led our team in creating a game that we named "Ready Poong".

One sentence description for our app ‚ÄúReady Poong‚Äù will be ‚ÄúBeing Ready to deal with next typoon!‚Äù.

The purpose of our app is to make entertaining educational apps that allow users to have fun while they learn how to prepare for a typhoon in 3 stages. In this game, people have to complete every stage within a set amount of time.

Our target user will be people who want to learn how to prepare for a typhoon with fun, people who want to teach how to prepare for a typhoon and people who may experience a typhoon. Ultimately, Everyone can be a user.


Game Engine: Unity3D Engine
VR Platform: Google Cardboard
Device: Android
Language: C#

Features

  • We put various sound effects into the game. We put 3D sound on the radio to change the sound volume according to distance. Users can hear water sound when they turn on the water, taping sound when they have to stick tape on the window and pick up sound when they pick up some objects.

  • Second, we use the creative method which is looking up the ceiling to see the pause, quit, and restart buttons. We thought this method would not be a big obstacle in playing the game as it also complements the feature of cardboard that only uses one button.

  • In addition, there are three stages and three bad endings, so users can enjoy more diverse content. It's more entertaining because the games on each stage are different, and it's fun to see multiple endings because different bad endings come out in different situations. We think some users will play the game multiple times to see all possible endings.

  • Our game puts the instruction in the middle so that the user can easily learn how to play the game. Before you start this game, the tutorial will show you, for example, how to move. And before you start each stage, it will tell you what to do at each stage. If you forget what to do during the game, you can check again by looking at the TV asset in the game.

  • We tried to create a realistic gaming environment to help the user's immersion. Especially the water graphic coming out in stage 1 is very realistic. Also, the graphic of rain and trees swaying boldly by the wind reminds the user that the situation in the game is a serious situation that will soon cause a typhoon.
